This is certainly the standout fixture this weekend in La Liga. Real Madrid’s shock Copa del Rey exit to Leganes in midweek has piled on the pressure on Zinedine Zidane who opted to leave numerous key players out in that one and firmly paid the price. It could end up costing him his job in the summer or perhaps even sooner if this ends up a trophy-less season for Los Blancos, besides their Spanish Super Cup triumph back in August. Just about everything has gone wrong since then and they certainly won’t be collecting any more domestic silverware this term. They come into this one a staggering 19 points behind Barcelona and 5 behind Valencia, although they do have a game in hand. If things don’t pick up soon, they could face a battle just to make the top four.

Despite all that, the bookies still have Real in as short as 8/13 for the win here, which is crazy when you consider the sort of season Valencia have been having, particularly at the Mestalla. Los Che have won 70% of their home league games and average 2.3 points per game in front of their own fans. Only Barcelona can better that and they are one of the few sides to have taken points off the Catalan giants this term.

They’ve also already taken points off the two big Madrid clubs, so they’ve shown they are capable of rising to the occasion and their 2-2 draw at the Bernabeu back in August no longer looks as strange a result as it did at the time. They did suffer a setback last weekend at Las Palmas in a game they finished with 9 men but it was only their 4th league defeat of the campaign and they’ve only lost 1 of their 13 competitive home games this term. Real Madrid come into this with just 2 wins from their last 6 in all competitions and they have issues at both ends of the pitch. While they did dismantle a poor Depor side last weekend, they’ve only scored 3 goals in their last 4 away league games, winning none of them.

You have to go all the way back to 14th October for Real Madrid’s last away league win, a narrow victory at Getafe and given that, there looks to be fantastic value in backing Valencia Double Chance here.

The hosts do have one or two defensive selection headaches though, largely due to an undisciplined display in the Canary Islands last weekend, which should see Real Madrid get chances but Valencia are pretty much at full strength elsewhere. They are the 2nd highest scorers in La Liga with 2.05 goals per game and backing Valencia to Win & Both Teams to Score looks well worth a flutter here too.
